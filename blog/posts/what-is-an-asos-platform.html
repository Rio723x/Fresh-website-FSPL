What an ASOS platform actually is

In consumer electronics, good after-sales service is quietly becoming one of the strongest selling propositions a brand has, not just a cost center to manage.

An ASOS platform is the operating layer that runs a brand's entire after-sales operation as one connected system, rather than the usual patchwork of call centers, spreadsheets, WhatsApp groups, and disconnected technician apps.

The analogy is to an operating system: just as an OS coordinates hardware, processes, and applications under one runtime, an ASOS coordinates everything that happens after a product is sold.

The gain is as much about what disappears as what gets added.

What disappears with an ASOS

1. Data silos

Instead of customer history sitting in one tool, technician notes in another, and parts and warranty data in a third, it all lives on one record that everyone works off.

2. Dependence on third-party service centers

That add time at every handoff while owning none of the outcome. When the brand runs the flow directly with its own team, accountability sits with the brand instead of a routing chain nobody fully controls.

Why it matters most in consumer electronics

This matters most in consumer electronics, where the larger goods carry the slowest service. An AC or a refrigerator complaint usually means an on-site visit, the right spare part in hand, and often a second trip when the diagnosis or the part is wrong. Each handoff between the call center, the technician, and the parts desk adds delay.

An ASOS collapses those handoffs into one flow, so the brand routes the right technician with the right part and context on the first visit instead of the third.

The revenue impact: AMC & EWS renewals

It also shows up directly in revenue. When every service event is structured data, the brand knows exactly whose warranty or AMC is about to lapse and can prompt the renewal at the right moment instead of letting it slip.

A connected after-sales layer turns AMC and EWS renewals from a leaky afterthought into a tracked, recurring revenue line.

The timing: Urban Company's move

Urban Company entered consumer durables in October 2023 under its own brand, Native, starting with water purifiers and later adding smart door locks. It is now reported to be planning an air-conditioner-cum-air-purifier ahead of Diwali this year, as it expands its consumer durables vertical, (Magzter) though the company has not officially confirmed the launch.

“

They clearly treat strong service as a selling proposition in itself: their Native products are positioned as integrated and backed by strong service, on the view that even when servicing is rare, consumers want the assurance that help is available quickly if needed.

– Forbes India

The signal for OEMs

The same platform that already sits inside millions of homes for service is now selling appliances of its own. A brand that does not control its own after-sales slowly hands the customer relationship to whoever shows up to fix the product.

An OEM that already runs its after-sales as one connected system keeps that relationship, and walks into a far more competitive market with the service moat already built, instead of scrambling to build it after Diwali.

Final takeaway: ASOS is not just a support tool. It is the operating system for after-sales. It drives better service, stronger loyalty, and a more profitable business.
