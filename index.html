Our Philosophy

The Service Industry Doesn't Have a People Problem. It Has a Systems Problem.

“Henry Ford borrowed an idea from Chicago's meatpacking plants: conveyor belts that brought the work to the worker. He turned it into the assembly line, cutting car build time from 12 hours to just 90 minutes. When the repetitive work made people quit, he doubled their wages, not out of generosity, but to keep workers and lift output. Efficiency became a self-reinforcing system of growth.”
