Our Philosophy

This isn't new thinking. It's proven thinking — applied to service ops.

The most powerful operational revolutions in history didn't happen by working harder. They happened by redesigning the system itself.

Henry Ford took inspiration from meatpacking plants in Chicago, where conveyor belts moved work to stationary workers. He flipped this into the assembly line — cutting car production time from 12 hours to just 90 minutes by simplifying tasks and creating flow. When repetitive work caused high turnover, he doubled wages — not out of generosity, but to stabilize workers and boost output — turning efficiency into a self-reinforcing system of growth.

— The Ford Assembly Line Principle, 1913

Ford didn't just build cars faster. He redesigned the system around the work — so every step fed the next without friction, waste, or human error slowing things down.

That's exactly what Fixolutions does for after-sales service. We don't patch your existing process. We give you a system with flow — where complaint booking feeds dispatch, dispatch feeds job tracking, tracking feeds parts, parts feed billing, and billing feeds AMC renewal.

Every step automated. Every stage accountable. Every outcome measurable.

System Diagnosis

Six failure modes in manual service operations.

Every unstructured service backend bleeds money, time, and trust. Here's where.

Critical
Manual Technician Assignment
Coordinators manually match jobs to techs — causing dispatch delays, misallocation, and zero accountability on failure.
Critical
Zero Real-Time Visibility
No live view of job stage, technician location, or SLA status — issues surface only when customers escalate.
High
Excessive Turnaround Time
Delayed dispatch, unclear job stages, no escalation triggers — customers wait longer than they should and churn.
Critical
AMC / EWS Revenue Leakage
Renewal opportunities slip through. No automated nudges, no pipeline tracking, no upsell system in place.
High
Spare Parts Leakage
Parts go untracked and misattributed. No digital trail means no audit, no control, no cost recovery.
Medium
Single-Point Coordinator Dependency
Ops relies on 1–2 people. When they're unavailable, the system stops. No redundancy, no scale path.

Execution Workflow

Two-phase job execution.

Most platforms treat on-site service as a single black box. Fixolutions splits it into distinct, accountable stages — creating structure where none existed.

PHASE 1

Diagnose

The technician arrives, assesses the appliance, and logs a structured diagnosis — before any fix begins.

  • Technician confirms arrival (timestamp logged)
  • Root cause identified and recorded
  • Spare parts requirement flagged
  • Customer updated in real-time through the company's consumer communication channel
PHASE 2

Fix

Only after diagnosis is logged does the technician proceed to resolve — with full accountability on outcome.

  • Repair executed against diagnosis record
  • Parts usage logged and verified
  • Digital service report auto-generated
  • AMC/EWS renewal nudge triggered
  • Better data captured for technician performance improvement
